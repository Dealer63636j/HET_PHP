BMW G310 RR

Details

The BMW G 310 RR is crafted for those who want a race-inspired experience in a lightweight, accessible package. This bike combines BMW Motorrad's legendary engineering with sleek, aerodynamic design, making it perfect for urban streets or open highways. The BMW G 310 RR delivers a thrilling ride, powered by a 300 CC engine that offers remarkable speed and control, perfect for both beginners and experienced riders alike. The design of the BMW G 310 RR is characterised by motorsport: for a sporty appearance. The agile single-cylinder engine, Ride by Wire, the anti-hopping clutch and the various riding modes are designed for sheer riding pleasure. The BMW G310RR clearly showcases its S 1000 RR heritage. Simply put: #NeverStopChallenging.

BMW M5

Details

The BMW M5 is a super high-performance variant of the BMW 5 Series marketed under the BMW M sub-brand. It is considered an iconic vehicle in the sports saloon category.[1][2] The M5 has always been produced in the saloon (sedan, US English) body style, but in some countries the M5 has also been available as an estate/touring (wagon, US English) from 1992 to 1995, from 2006 to 2010, and since 2024.[3][4][5] The first M5 model was hand-built beginning in late 1984 on the E28 535i chassis with a modified engine from the M1 that made it the fastest production saloon at the time.[6] M5 models have been produced for every generation of the 5 Series since 1984, with occasional gaps in production (1995 to 1998, 2023 to 2024).

BMW M8 Coupe Competition

Details

The BMW M8 is the high-performance version of the BMW 8 Series (G15) marketed under the BMW M sub-brand. Introduced in June 2019, the M8 was initially produced in the 2-door convertible (F91 model code) and 2-door coupe (F92 model code) body styles.[3][4] A 4-door sedan (F93 model code, marketed as 'Gran Coupe') body style was added to the lineup in October 2019.[5][6][7][8] The M8 is powered by the BMW S63 twin-turbocharged V8 engine shared with the BMW M5 (F90).
